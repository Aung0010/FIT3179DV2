Malaysia Tourism & GDP

National Overview

How Tourism Spending Moved with Malaysia’s GDP?

  • Before 2020, there was a clear pattern and a strong positive correlation between tourism expenditure and GDP.

  • In 2021, GDP rose but tourism expenditure remained low, suggesting that the economy had begun recovering while borders were still closed.

  • GDP reached its lowest point in 2020 due to the global economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, while tourism expenditure hit its minimum in 2021 when international travel restrictions persisted.

Where did the visitors come from?

  • Most visitors originated from neighbouring Asian countries and Australia, reflecting Malaysia’s strong regional travel connections.

  • Across the years, Singapore, Indonesia, Thailand, China, Brunei and India consistently emerged as Malaysia’s top visitor sources.

  • Tourism inflows are heavily Asia-centric, suggesting that Malaysia could broaden its promotion to capture emerging non-Asian markets in the future.

Note: Origin (blue) points are based on major flight cities.

Which states stood out in 2023?

  • Neighbouring countries favoured border states: Singapore → Johor, Thailand → Kedah/Perlis , and Brunei → Sabah, reflecting strong cross-border tourism.

  • China tourists concentrated in Sabah, driven by distinctive Borneo attractions, direct flights and short travel distance..

  • Kuala Lumpur and Selangor stood out as major transport and urban hubs with strong shopping and airport connectivity.

Note: Values indicate each country's in-country visitor share, not Malaysia’s total arrivals.

  • States with high GDP, such as Selangor and Kuala Lumpur, did not necessarily have the most tourists.

  • Johor and Sabah attracted large visitor numbers despite lower GDP, reflecting border proximity and natural appeal.

  • Rising tourist flows in smaller economies like Perlis and Kedah show that accessibility and niche attractions drive demand more than GDP size.

  • Peneng had high GDP despite lower number of tourists.
How Tourist Spending Priorities Have Shifted Over Time?
  • Shopping consistently accounted for the largest share of tourist spending each year, reflecting Malaysia’s strong retail appeal.

  • Shopping, accommodation, food & beverages, and transportation together made up the majority of total tourist expenditure throughout the period.

  • Accommodation spending showed a steady recovery after 2022, aligning with the reopening of international borders.

Note : Data for 2021 is omitted due to insufficient records during the lockdown period.
